🤖 Knowledge Terminal

Beginner-friendly answers to common questions about space exploration.

Auroras form in the upper atmosphere, and astronauts aboard spacecraft and the ISS can sometimes see them from above.
Auroras are created when charged particles from the Sun (solar wind) interact with a planet's magnetic field and atmosphere.
Different gases produce different colors. Oxygen can create green or red auroras, while nitrogen can produce blue and purple shades.
Most auroras occur between 60 km and 500 km above Earth's surface.
Jupiter, Saturn, Mars, and several other planets experience auroras, though they may look very different from Earth's.
Astronauts often photograph auroras from orbit, where they appear as glowing curtains surrounding the planet.
Rockets generate massive thrust using powerful engines. When the thrust becomes stronger than Earth’s gravitational pull, the rocket lifts off and travels into space.
Astronauts float in space not because there is no gravity, but because they are in a constant state of free fall around the Earth, a condition known as microgravity. Both the astronauts and their spacecraft are falling towards Earth at the same rate, allowing them to feel weightless.
Stars form inside massive clouds of gas and dust called nebulae. Gravity pulls the material together until the center becomes hot enough for nuclear fusion to begin.
A mission to Mars usually takes around 6 to 9 months depending on the spacecraft’s speed and the positions of Earth and Mars in their orbits.
Astronauts sleep inside sleeping bags attached to walls or ceilings so they do not float around in zero gravity.
Astronauts do not take regular showers in space. They use rinseless shampoo, wet towels, and small amounts of water for cleaning.
Earth appears blue because most of its surface is covered by oceans, and the atmosphere scatters blue light from the Sun.
Satellites help with communication, weather forecasting, GPS navigation, scientific research, and observing Earth from space.
Mars appears red because its surface contains iron oxide, also known as rust.
A black hole is a region in space with gravity so powerful that nothing, not even light, can escape it.
Astronauts eat specially prepared food that can survive zero gravity, including dehydrated meals, tortillas, fruits, and packaged snacks.
Saturn currently has the highest known number of moons in our solar system.
