CHANDRAYAAN-3 PHYSICS & ANATOMY SCRIPT

1. The Structure: How It Was Put Together

Chandrayaan-3 was built using a Modular Configuration — two distinct modules stacked together, total launch mass 3,900 kg.

  • Module A: Propulsion Module (PM) — 2,148 kg (of which ~1,696 kg was propellant). Acted as a celestial ferry carrying the lander to a 100 km lunar orbit. Carried the SHAPE payload to study Earth as an exoplanet analog.
  • Module B: Vikram Lander — 1,752 kg. Aluminum-Lithium box frame, titanium legs handling up to 2 m/s touchdown, extra solar panels.
  • Module C: Pragyan Rover — 26 kg. 6-wheeled rocker-bogie suspension tucked inside Vikram's belly.

2. Fast Facts About How It Was Made

  • Crash-Proof Brain: Autonomous Landing Sequence (ALS) computer ran ISRO algorithms for the final 15 minutes of descent — no Earth commands.
  • The Laser Eye: Laser Doppler Velocimeter (LDV) on Vikram's belly measured descent speed by bouncing laser beams off the lunar ground.
  • Zero Central Thruster: Removed the center engine vs. Chandrayaan-2, simplifying plumbing and reducing dry weight.

3. Important Physical Laws and Core Properties Used

  • A. Kepler's Laws & Oberth Effect: Firing engines at perigee (maximum velocity) yielded maximum kinetic energy gain per kg of fuel, progressively stretching the orbit.
  • B. Newton's Laws & Throttling: Hypergolic MMH/MON3 propellants generated thrust. As fuel burned and mass decreased, engines throttled down dynamically to maintain constant deceleration under F=ma.
  • C. Thermal Dynamics & Materials: Gold/amber aluminized Mylar MLI blankets reflected heat. Titanium alloy legs absorbed landing impact; Aluminum-Lithium alloy kept chassis mass low.
